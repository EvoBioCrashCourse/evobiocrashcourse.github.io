Introduction

Representation matters, especially for young students from the Global South who want to start a career in science. However, systemic knowledge gaps persist and cause lags outside of North America and Europe, making access to those fields much more difficult for some prospective students. Evolutionary Biology is not an exception, despite ongoing efforts to increase inclusion. We created an Evolutionary Biology Crash Course to help prospective students achieve a better understanding of the field and increase their chances to have access to high level master's programmes and an opportunity at a career as future evolutionary biologists. Our first edition will be held online from July 11th to 23rd, 2022.

Registration

The registration for the first edition of the EvoBio Crash Course is open until the 10th of June, 2022. You can register here!
The course will take place completely online and will last for two weeks. We are trying to accommodate for students in different time zones, prioritizing those in the Global South, so live Q&A sessions will be held twice a day. It is free and we will not ask for any entry requirements, other than an interest for Evolutionary Biology. Keep in mind, however, that the course is designed for undergraduate students with a background in Biological Sciences. We will hand out certificates of completion to those students that assist to at least 50% of Q&A sessions and hand in all of the practical exercises.

We are supported by the Erasmus Mundus Master in Evolutionary Biology (MEME) and funded by the Equal Opportunities Initiative Fund of the European Society of Evolutionary Biology (ESEB).
